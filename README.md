# Qatar North Field: Critical Analysis of Official Reserve Disclosures

**An independent petroleum engineering assessment of Qatar's reserve statements against SEC Regulation S-X Rule 4-10 and SPE-PRMS 2018 standards**

## Overview

This report presents a systematic assessment of Qatar's official reserve statements for the North Field gas accumulation—the world's largest non-associated gas field—against independent material balance analysis (MBAL) and SEC-compliant reserves classification standards.

### Key Findings

- **Qatar's official "proved reserves" of ~900 Tcf appear to violate SEC definitions**, remaining static despite 258 Tcf of cumulative production (1991–2024)
- **Independent MBAL analysis yields significantly different estimates**: ~1,290 Tcf OGIP, with SEC 2P proved reserves ranging from 60–134 Tcf depending on development stage
- **Condensate reserve claims exceed physical constraints**: The 2024 claim of ">80 billion barrels" exceeds the independently-assessed GIIP-c ceiling of 51.6 billion barrels by ~55%
- **Systematic inconsistencies in classification and disclosure** across four decades of public statements

## Methodology

The independent assessment uses:

- **Volumetric OGIP calculation** from publicly available structure maps and net pay data
- **p/z material balance analysis** incorporating production and pressure data
- **CGR phase-behaviour analysis** for condensate reserve assessment
- **Two-phase Z-factor correction** for gas-condensate systems
- **SEC Regulation S-X Rule 4-10** and **SPE-PRMS 2018** as classification frameworks

Full technical methodology is documented in the companion paper: [SSRN 6513138](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=6513138)
