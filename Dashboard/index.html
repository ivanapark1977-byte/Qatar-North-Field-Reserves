Notes on production model: The existing 14 LNG trains plus Dolphin Pipeline and GTL plants contribute a base rate of ~18.5 Bcf/d as at end-2025. Without the North Field Production Sustainability (NFPS) compression programme, natural reservoir pressure decline (estimated ~4%/yr from 2026) would erode this base. NFPS (COMP 1–4, ~US$10 billion) is modelled as a sustain offset — it arrests the natural decline of the base trains, not as an incremental addition. NFE, NFS, NFW and subsequent phases represent genuine incremental capacity. The "without NFPS" counterfactual (dashed red) shows the base-train decline trajectory absent compression investment.

Recovery factor 80% (base; Bahrain Khuff analogue SPE-13679: 72% at 1,500 psia abandonment; NFPS compression enables lower abandonment). Cumulative production to end-2025: ~130 Tcf. Average reservoir pressure: ~4,050 psia (~76% of initial 5,300 psia), below estimated dew point ~4,500 psia. Classification per SPE-PRMS 2018: NFE & NFS = Proved Undeveloped (FID taken); NFW = Contingent Resources 1C–2C (pre-FID); additional phases = Contingent 2C–3C.
